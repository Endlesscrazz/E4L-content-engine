In a large-scale comparative study carried out by the Centre for Biofield Sciences under the supervision of Dr. Thornton Streeter, 96.5% of the 240 participants in the control group experienced a positive change (compared to 35% in the placebo group).

Infoceuticals Shown to Improve Mean Arterial Pressure Readings
 
A case study of 5 participants explored the impact of Energy4Life Infoceuticals on the health and wellbeing of people with a variety of physical complaints. This was undertaken as a master’s degree research project under the supervision of Professor Anna van Wersch for the University of Teesside.

The Infoceuticals had a positive physiological effect throughout the intervention period as determined using mean arterial pressure readings, which measures blood flow, resistance, and pressure with the arteries, and is an important indicator of cardiovascular health.

Energy4Life Infoceuticals Significantly Improved Quality of Life, Increase Body Lean Mass, and Reduce Body Fat

Seven overweight/obese (1 male and 6 female) volunteers, mean age 42 years, participated in a two month case study, in which participants followed Infoceutical protocols without any change in diet or exercise.
At the end of two months, all participants reported higher IWQOL-Lite (quality of life) scores than their baseline scores (changes ranged from 1.9-18.3), and four of the seven participants demonstrated clinically meaningful change. Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis (BIA) revealed a decreased body fat mass index (BFFM) and body fat percentage and an increase of their lean mass, fat free mass index (FFMI) and body water respectively.

The Use of the E4L miHealth for Shift Workers

A clinical study involving 6 participants examined whether use of the E4L miHealth would lead to a reduction in the physical problems associated with working nightshifts.
A trend of improvement of most vitality signs was observed, with data showing an improvement in sleep, weight control and energy parameters. The best result was that, after the miHealth intervention, the subjects were better able to make the change from nightshifts to dayshifts, and reported that the nightshift was easier and involved less energy and effort.

2025 Elsevier Published Findings on Infoceuticals – Summary of Results:

Title:
Protective effect of energized structured water on bioenergetic function and oxidative stress in H9c2 cells

URL / link to the paper online:
https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1550830725001235

Key Points:
* Energized Structured Water (ESW):
Infoceuticals were referred to as ESW in this paper and described as water that has been imbued with energetic frequencies or vibrations, leading to enhanced molecular organization and possible health benefits.
* Study Focus:
The research examined the impact of ESW on cellular bioenergetics (energy production at the cellular level), oxidative stress, and gene expression in H9c2 cells (a rat heart muscle cell line).

Methods:
* H9c2 cells were pretreated with either ESW or control water (CW).
* Mitochondrial function was assessed, as well as cell viability and reactive oxygen species (ROS) levels, especially under induced oxidative stress (with H₂O₂).
* Gene expression analysis and Gene Ontology (GO) enrichment were performed to evaluate biological processes affected by ESW.

Major Results:
1. Enhanced Mitochondrial Function:
* ESW pretreatment significantly increased maximal and spare respiratory capacity in H9c2 cells (indicating greater energy-producing ability).
* Glycolytic ATP production increased with ESW, although mitochondrial and total ATP production were not significantly altered.
2. Cell Protection:
* ESW treatment improved cell viability and reduced ROS production (less oxidative stress) when cells were exposed to H₂O₂-induced oxidative stress.
3. Gene Expression:
* ESW altered the expression of 1,139 genes: 717 were upregulated (including genes like Myh1 [involved in muscle contraction and energy-demanding processes], Akr1b8 [involved in detoxification and protection against oxidative stress], Hmox1 [involved in protection against oxidative damage, etc.), and 422 were downregulated.
* GO analysis showed significant effects on cellular stress response pathways and energy metabolism.
